1 ἄνδρα μοι ἔννεπε, μοῦσα, πολύτροπον, ὃς μάλα πολλὰ Tell me, Muse, of the man of many ways, Tell me about a complicated man.
2 πλάγχθη, ἐπεὶ Τροίης ἱερὸν πτολίεθρον ἔπερσεν: who was driven far journeys, after he had sacked Troy’s sacred citadel. Muse, tell me how he wandered and was lost
3 πολλῶν δ᾽ ἀνθρώπων ἴδεν ἄστεα καὶ νόον ἔγνω, Many were they whose cities he saw, whose minds he learned of, when he had wrecked the holy town of Troy,
4 πολλὰ δ᾽ ὅ γ᾽ ἐν πόντῳ πάθεν ἄλγεα ὃν κατὰ θυμόν, many the pains he suffered in his spirit on the wide sea, and where he went, and who he met,
5 ἀρνύμενος ἥν τε ψυχὴν καὶ νόστον ἑταίρων. struggling for his own life and the homecoming of his companions. the pain he suffered on the sea,
6 ἀλλ᾽ οὐδ᾽ ὣς ἑτάρους ἐρρύσατο, ἱέμενός περ: Even so he could not save his companions, hard though and how he worked to save his life and bring his men back home.
7 αὐτῶν γὰρ σφετέρῃσιν ἀτασθαλίῃσιν ὄλοντο, they were destroyed by their own wild recklessness, He failed, and for their own mistakes, they died.
8 νήπιοι, οἳ κατὰ βοῦς Ὑπερίονος Ἠελίοιο fools, who devoured the oxen of Helios, the Sun God, They are the Sun God's cattle, and the god kept them from home.
9 ἤσθιον: αὐτὰρ ὁ τοῖσιν ἀφείλετο νόστιμον ἦμαρ. and he took away the day of their homecoming. Now goddess, child of Zeus, tell the old story for our modern times.
10 From some point here, goddess, daughter of Zeus, speak, and begin our story. Find the beginning.
